What this crop trains

p_user_has_floor and the known p_assistant_has_floor input are soft turn-state estimates. Continuation pauses retain the floor at reduced confidence; backchannels do not assert floor ownership. p_user_yield predicts that the user will not hold the floor 500 ms ahead and is supervised only within 500 ms of a user speech offset.

At runtime, the assistant-floor phase reads p_user_has_floor to hand control to the user. Once the user owns the floor, the user-floor phase reads p_user_yield to release it; the floor-state output does not veto that transition. p_assistant_backchannel predicts an annotated assistant backchannel during the next 200 ms, allowing a runtime policy to start generating it before its acoustic onset. Only recurrent burn-in, censored lookahead, and unsupported standalone audio-only regions remove runtime loss.

Auxiliary supervision

Future activity is a soft occupancy fraction for four future horizons, not a hard speech/no-speech label and not a pause classifier. Continuation-pause and non-floor feedback targets retain the source annotation's probability across the full annotated span. Turn completion and floor take / interruption remain causal point targets on one frame. Frames without an applicable auxiliary label are blank and contribute no auxiliary loss. Ambiguous annotations, censored horizons, padding, and the four-second recurrent burn-in contribute no loss.
